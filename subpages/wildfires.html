Wildfire Risk Assessment

In recent years, forest fires in British Columbia have affected ecosystems, displaced communities, and have contributed to adverse health conditions. The purpose of this project was to develop a method for estimating the burn intensity of recent forest fires and to track trends in a selection of fire risk factors.

Findings support current remote sensing literature stating that warmer temperatures, drier vegetation, and sloped topography are associated with higher wildfire risk. The project also adds to literature that exemplifies how remote sensing can be used for forest fire analysis. The results may guide provincial aerial fire management.
