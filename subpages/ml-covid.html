Data Mining for COVID-19 Outcome Predictions GitHub

For my Data Mining course project, my team and I work on the objective of constructing an optimal classification model to accurately categorize the health outcomes (hospitalized, non-hospitalized, recovered, deceased) of COVID-19 patients, drawing insights from publicly available datasets. Our project's framework encompassed three distinct phases: data preprocessing, establishment of baseline models, and subsequent optimization through hyperparameter tuning, guided by the search for the most effective predictive model.

Delving into exploratory analyses, I uncovered critical insights that informed our modeling strategies and decision-making processes. In this endeavor, we curated data, performed visual data analysis, and evaluated various metrics to ensure the robustness and generalizability of our final model. Emphasizing the importance of minimizing false negatives, particularly in predicting deceased cases, our efforts centered on achieving a balanced performance across accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score metrics.

Through these initiatives, I have not only expanded my technical skills but also strengthened my ability to apply data-driven methodologies to address contemporary issues.
